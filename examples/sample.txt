Lady Marguise pursed her lips, but I actually welcomed the distraction and almost jumped out of the carriage to take a deep breath. The air felt like a theater waiting for the curtain to fall – dramatic, true, but mostly just annoying. Even the fish smell seemed to go quiet for a moment. I looked up and saw storm clouds gathering above. A flock of Auraflights was flying West, towards Wildvale Hills probably.

I saw Avien was already sitting on a rock next to the river, so I approached him.

"Brother Avien," I raised my hand in a greeting.

He smiled without moving his eyes from the river. "What is it Lady Anette? Still worried about Ar?"

"Of course I am," I said standing nearby – my dress wasn't exactly made for sitting on rocks. "How much of what they say about the Republic is true?" I asked, following his gaze.

He grabbed a rock, weighted it in his hand and threw it into the river, making it skip a few times. "Less than you fear. But more than I would like."

I sighed. "That means you don't know, right?"

He grinned and finally looked at me. "I don't," he admitted. "Never was to Ar myself. But I know people who were. And they did confirm many things."
